I recently coded the runic of paralysis. Like other runics, it has an arithmetic probability of triggering, and when a monster is hit by a runic of paralysis, he is paralyzed for x turns, where x is the runic’s level (initially 1, and incremented by 1 every time you apply a runic of paralysis or a runic of boost). Then, hitting a paralyzed monster deals triple damage, so after paralyzing a monster; you should finish him!

As explained in the potion of fury post, I wanted to avoid bloating the interface to display the status of paralyzed monsters. Hence I apply a transparent layer whose color is the one of the paralysis runic to paralyzed monsters. This does not consume any space, which is nice when playing on a phone. Here’s a video showing a rogue using a dagger of paralysis against a pack of orcs:

When the rogue is paralyzed, the effect is similar to the potion of fury. A global transparent layer is applied:

While paralyzed, the implementation of the rogue’s turn is simply waiting for 100 milliseconds, so that you can see the monsters moving around you. Here’s an example screenshot:

In the video, I’ve equipped every orc with a weapon of +10 paralysis so they are very deadly, because as soon as they hit you, you’re paralyzed for 10 turns, and while paralyzed, you’re automatically hit.. I suppose I’ll add a weak monster that wields a weapon with paralysis, such as a Medusa.
