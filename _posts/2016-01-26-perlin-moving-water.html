I recently coded display of water.

As usual, I wanted to do something at least as nice as brogue (as shown in this video). And as usual, there was stuff to do that already in the amazing SquidLib. To display terrain that often changes, SquidLib uses Perlin noise. At every libGDX call to the game’s render method, cells whose terrain changes (water, lava, mud; in other words: liquids) are retrieved and their color is slightly changed by Perlin-generated noise. What is nice is that Perlin noise makes a smooth change over time, really as if - considering this is ASCII art.. - the terrain was gently changing.
